Selected Research

IBM T.J. Watson Research Center · ibm_kyiv, ibm_torino

Bayesian-Augmented Zero-Noise Extrapolation (BZNE)

A framework combining classical-shadow-informed priors with Bayesian zero-noise extrapolation to mitigate error on real superconducting quantum hardware, developed in collaboration with a research scientist at IBM T.J. Watson Research Center. Tested on H₂ and LiH VQE problems.

H₂ showed a 94.1% reduction in error versus the unmitigated result. LiH performed worse than the unmitigated baseline — a result traced to biased priors, and a useful negative result in its own right.

Placed 3rd, Computer Science at WESEF; submitted to Regeneron STS.

with E. Gentry, A. Joseph, A. Schofill

Hedging Language in arXiv Abstracts and Citation ImpactIn Progress

A statistical study of how hedging language in physics preprint abstracts relates to downstream citation counts, built on a full data pipeline (arXiv metadata, Semantic Scholar citation retrieval) and written in ACM two-column conference format.

Can Cosmic Rays Degrade a Quantum Sensor?Proposal

A proposal to study GeV hadron irradiation of NV-center diamond quantum sensors at the CERN T9 beamline, with post-irradiation characterization support from Prof. Carlos Meriles (CUNY City College). The team did not place, but the work led to an invitation from Prof. Meriles for a funded summer lab visit on radiation trajectory visualization in diamond.
